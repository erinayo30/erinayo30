About my School

      The story of Adekunle Ajasin University Akungba (AAUA),Ondo State,Nigeria is more approriately told in two phases. When it was established in March 1982, It was named Obafemi Awolowo University, by the government of the Old Ondo State, led by the late Chief Adekunle Ajasin. The suceedring mliltary government of Navy Commodore Bamidele Otiko changed the name to Ondo State University in 1985

Establishment at Akungba

>

      Relocation to Akungba Akoko became imperative in 1999 following the creation of a new state out of the Old Ondo State three years ealier in October 1996. The bill for the relocation was signed into law by the Governor of the state Chief Adebayo Adefarati, precisely on November 5,1999.Prompting the movement of a crop of workers from the old site in Ado-Ekiti to Akungba-Akoko on December 1,1999

The bill for yet another change of name, this time to Adekunle Ajasin University after the late Governor Ajasinwas signed into law by Governor Adefarati in 2004. The amended and subsisting Statue of the University was signed into law in November2007, by former Governor Olusegun Agagu.

At present there are over 15,000 students and over 1200 academic and non academic staff. There are seven Faculties in the school which Agriculture, Arts, Education,Environmental, Law, Science, Social and Managememt Science. All programmes offered are fully acreditated by the Nigeria University Commission
